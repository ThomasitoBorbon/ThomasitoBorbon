Popular events in Tokyo

  1. Cherry Blossom Season - From late March to early April, Tokyo's parks and streets are filled with the beautiful pink and white blooms of cherry blossoms.

  2. Summer Festivals - Summer in Tokyo is filled with lively festivals, or matsuri, featuring colorful parades, traditional performances, and fireworks displays. Some of the most popular festivals include the Sumida River Fireworks Festival, the Koenji Awa Odori Festival, and the Asakusa Samba Carnival .

  3. Tokyo Game Show - Held annually in September, the Tokyo Game Show is one of the largest video game expos in the world, featuring the latest in gaming technology, software, and merchandise

  4. Tokyo International Film Festival - This annual film festival, held in October, showcases the best of Japanese and international cinema, with screenings, events, and talks by industry professionals.

  5. New Year's Celebrations - Tokyo celebrates the New Year with a variety of traditional customs and events, including visiting shrines and temples for hatsumode (the first shrine visit of the year), watching the first sunrise, and eating special New Year's dishes like ozoni soup.

Spring

Spring is one of the most popular seasons to visit Tokyo, as the city is filled with beautiful cherry blossoms and a festive atmosphere. From late March to early April, Tokyo's parks and streets are covered in pink and white blooms of cherry blossoms, attracting both locals and tourists. During this time, people often hold hanami (cherry blossom viewing) parties, where they enjoy food and drinks under the blossoms. Some of the popular spots to see cherry blossoms in Tokyo include Ueno Park, Shinjuku Gyoen, and Chidorigafuchi .

Spring is also the season for many traditional Japanese festivals, or matsuri, including the Sanja Matsuri and Kanda Matsuri. These festivals feature lively parades with traditional costumes, music, and performances, and are a great way to experience Japanese culture.

Spring is extremely popular in Japan because of the cherry blossom trees and how iconic they are in pop culture because of their beauty and uniqueness,cherry blossoms have long been associated with Japanese culture, where they represent the beauty of life, the transience of existence, and the arrival of spring.

Winter

Tokyo Auto Salon

The Tokyo Auto Salon attracts car enthusiasts from all over the world, with over 300,000 visitors attending the event in recent years. The show is a great opportunity to see some of the world's most impressive automobiles, with exhibitors showcasing everything from classic cars to cutting-edge technology.

In addition to the cars themselves, visitors can enjoy a variety of events and exhibits, including drift demonstrations, race simulators, and product displays from leading automotive manufacturers. The Tokyo Auto Salon is a must-visit event for anyone with an interest in cars, offering a unique opportunity to see some of the world's most impressive vehicles up close and in person.

Autumn

Autumn in Tokyo is a wonderful time of year, with cooler temperatures and stunning foliage. The city is filled with vibrant colors, making it a great time to explore Tokyo's parks and gardens. There are many festivals and events happening throughout the season, including the Tokyo Game Show and the Kanda Matsuri.

Visitors can enjoy traditional Japanese food and sake during autumn festivals and take in the beautiful autumn scenery while strolling around the city. Whether you're a local or a visitor, autumn in Tokyo is not to be missed.

For those who love art and culture, autumn is a great time to visit Tokyo's many museums and galleries. The Tokyo National Museum , Mori Art Museum , and Edo-Tokyo Museum all offer special exhibitions and events during the fall season.

Summer

Mitama Matsuri

There are many festivals occuring during the Summer in Tokyo making it one of the popular seasons to visit.

Mitama Matsuri is a popular summer festival held annually at the Yasukuni Shrine in Tokyo. The festival features over 30,000 lanterns illuminating the shrine grounds and is dedicated to honoring the spirits of those who have died for Japan. Visitors can enjoy traditional Japanese music and dance performances, as well as food stalls selling a variety of delicious festival foods.

Sumida River Fireworks festival

The Sumida River Fireworks Festival is one of the most spectacular fireworks displays in Japan, held annually on the last Saturday of July. It features over 20,000 fireworks launched from barges on the Sumida River, creating a stunning show in the night sky. Millions of people flock to the riverside to watch the fireworks and enjoy the festive atmosphere, making it one of the largest and busiest summer events in Tokyo.

The sight of thousands of colorful fireworks lighting up the night sky is truly breathtaking. The festive atmosphere, delicious food, and lively music make it a fun and memorable experience for everyone.




Bon Odori Festival

The Bon Odori Festival is a traditional Japanese festival held in mid-August to honor the spirits of ancestors. During the festival, people wear yukata (summer kimono) and dance in circles around a yagura (raised platform) to the beat of traditional music. The festival also features food stalls, games, and other cultural performances, making it a fun and lively event for locals and visitors alike.
